🗣️ Parliamentary Research Tool

Search Hansard debates, Written Questions, and stakeholder activity on any topic. Generates an AI briefing from the parliamentary record — government and opposition positions, key speakers, and cited sources. Export to Word.

{# Stakeholder Research tab hidden until public launch #}

This tool draws together all parliamentary activity on a subject in one place. Choose a topic and date range — the AI will identify related themes and search Hansard on your behalf across Commons debates, Westminster Hall, the Lords, Written Questions, and Ministerial Statements simultaneously.

Before the full search runs, you can review and amend the AI-generated search terms using the Preview button. Results may take a moment to load, but will include an AI summary, government and opposition speakers, key questions raised, and the full list of relevant parliamentary business — all downloadable as a Word briefing.

ℹ️ How results are classified & relevance guide
Debate types
Parliamentary Debates — substantive speeches exceeding ~300 words. Includes Westminster Hall, Bill readings, and Statutory Instrument / Affirmative procedure debates.
Oral Questions — sessions where no speech exceeds 300 words (minister's prepared answer ~150 words, plus follow-up exchanges).
Urgent Questions — granted by the Speaker; minister responds immediately. Follow-ups included.
Written Ministerial Statements (WMS) — written statements published in Hansard by ministers, distinct from oral statements made at the despatch box.
Classification is automated and approximate. Use the ↗ Hansard link to verify.
Relevance indicators Highly relevant — 3 or more direct keyword matches in this session Moderate — 1–2 keyword matches; topic mentioned but not the main focus Low — no direct keyword matches; found via minister search or session expansion
